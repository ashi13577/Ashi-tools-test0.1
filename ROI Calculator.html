What is Return on Investment (ROI)?

Return on Investment (ROI) is a performance measure used to evaluate the efficiency or profitability of an investment or compare the efficiency of several different investments. It directly measures the amount of return on a particular investment, relative to the investment’s cost.

Why is ROI Used?

  • Investment Evaluation: Helps decide whether to pursue an investment by comparing potential returns.
  • Performance Measurement: Assesses how well an investment has performed over time.
  • Comparative Analysis: Allows for easy comparison between different investment opportunities.
  • Resource Allocation: Guides where to best allocate capital for maximum returns.

Demo Example: Marketing Campaign ROI

To calculate a basic ROI:

  • Net Gain from Investment: $20,000 (Revenue from campaign - Cost of campaign)
  • Cost of Investment: $5,000 (Total capital invested in campaign)
