Create a comprehensive marketing mix strategy using the extended 7Ps framework.

Product

What are you selling? Features, quality, branding, packaging, etc.

Price

How much will you charge? Pricing strategy, discounts, payment terms.

Place

Where will you sell? Distribution channels, locations, logistics.

Promotion

How will you communicate? Advertising, PR, social media, sales promotions.

People

Who delivers your service? Staff, recruitment, training, customer service.

Process

How is your service delivered? Customer journey, service blueprints, efficiency.

Physical Evidence

What tangible elements will customers see? Store design, ambiance, packaging.

About the 7Ps of Marketing

What are the 7Ps?

The 7Ps of Marketing is an extension of the traditional 4Ps marketing mix (Product, Price, Place, Promotion). The additional 3Ps (People, Process, Physical Evidence) were added to better address service marketing challenges.

When to Use?

The 7Ps framework is particularly useful for service-based businesses, but can benefit any business seeking a comprehensive marketing strategy. It helps ensure all aspects of the customer experience are considered.

How to Use?

For each of the 7 elements, define your strategy and tactics. Be specific about what makes your offering unique. Review and update your marketing mix regularly as market conditions change.
