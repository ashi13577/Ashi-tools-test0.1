What are Financial Ratios?

Financial ratios are powerful tools derived from a company's financial statements (Balance Sheet, Income Statement, Cash Flow Statement) to gain insights into its performance and health. They simplify complex financial data into digestible metrics for easy comparison and analysis.

Why are They Used?

  • Performance Assessment: Evaluate how efficiently a company is using its assets and managing its liabilities.
  • Trend Analysis: Track a company's performance over time to identify improvements or deteriorations.
  • Comparative Analysis: Benchmark a company's performance against industry averages or competitors.
  • Decision Making: Aid investors, creditors, and management in making informed decisions.

Categories Covered:

  • Liquidity Ratios: Measure a company's ability to meet its short-term obligations.
  • Profitability Ratios: Measure a company's ability to generate revenue and profit from its operations.

Profitability Ratios (Operating Performance)

Gross Profit Margin

Result: 40.00%

Formula: ((Net Sales - COGS) / Net Sales) * 100

Indicates the percentage of revenue left after deducting COGS. A higher margin is generally better, showing efficiency in production.

Operating Profit Margin

Result: 20.00%

Formula: (((Net Sales - COGS) - Operating Expenses) / Net Sales) * 100

Shows the profitability of a company's core operations before interest and taxes. A higher margin indicates better operational efficiency.

Net Profit Margin

Result: 16.00%

Formula: (Net Income / Net Sales) * 100

Indicates the percentage of revenue left after all expenses, including taxes and interest. A higher margin signifies strong overall profitability.

Return on Assets (ROA)

Result: 20.00%

Formula: (Net Income / Total Assets) * 100

Measures how efficiently a company uses its assets to generate earnings. A higher percentage is better.

Return on Equity (ROE)

Formula: (Net Income / Shareholder Equity) * 100

Indicates how much profit a company generates for each dollar of shareholders' equity. A higher percentage is generally good.
