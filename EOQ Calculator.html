Understanding Economic Order Quantity (EOQ)

Master the fundamentals of inventory optimization

What is EOQ?

Economic Order Quantity (EOQ) is the optimal number of units a company should purchase to minimize the total costs associated with ordering and holding inventory.

EOQ = √(2DS/H)

Where: D = Annual Demand, S = Ordering Cost, H = Holding Cost

Cost Components

Ordering Costs: Administrative costs, shipping, processing fees

Holding Costs: Storage, insurance, deterioration, opportunity cost

Total Cost: EOQ minimizes the sum of ordering and holding costs

Key Assumptions

• Demand is constant and known

• Lead time is constant

• No stockouts allowed

• Order quantity is received at once

• No quantity discounts

Related Formulas

Number of Orders per Year:
N = D / EOQ
Order Cycle Time:
T = EOQ / D × 365 days
Reorder Point:
ROP = (D / 365) × Lead Time

Significance & Benefits of EOQ

Why EOQ is crucial for business success

  • Cost Optimization: Minimizes total inventory costs by balancing ordering and holding expenses, leading to significant cost savings.
  • Cash Flow Management: Reduces excess inventory investment, freeing up capital for other business operations and growth opportunities.
  • Space Efficiency: Optimizes warehouse utilization by maintaining ideal inventory levels, reducing storage costs and improving organization.
  • Stockout Prevention: Ensures adequate inventory levels to meet customer demand while avoiding costly stockout situations.
  • Planning Efficiency: Provides systematic approach to inventory planning, reducing guesswork and improving supply chain coordination.
  • Supplier Relations: Enables better supplier relationship management through predictable and optimized ordering patterns.
  • Performance Metrics: Provides baseline for measuring inventory performance and identifying improvement opportunities.
  • Academic Foundation: Essential concept in operations management, supply chain, and business studies for strategic decision-making.
